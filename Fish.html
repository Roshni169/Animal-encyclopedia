Fish/ sea animals


There are over 500 species of sharks. The lifespan of a shark is 20-30 years in the wild. An adult shark can reach speeds of up till 50 km/h.

There is about 40 known species of Seahorse. They can camoflauge with their surroundings by changing the colour of their scales.

Jellyfish have no brain, heart, bones or eyes. They are made up of a smooth, bag-like body and tentacles armed with tiny, stinging cells.

Stingrays eyes are on top of their bodies, yet their mouth, gills, and nostrils are on the underside. Because of this stingrays cannot see their prey, instead they use smell to find their prey.

A starfish lives for around 35 years. They usually have 5 hands. Starfish also have no blood or brain. To escape predatrs, starfish can cut of their arms and grow them back when needed.

Frog fish are carnivores that use their amazing camoflauge ability to attract prey. Frog fish can live up till 20 years in the wild.
